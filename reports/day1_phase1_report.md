## Objective
Lock in the dataset, the primary metric, a reproducible train/test split, and a baseline leaderboard for an LLM hallucination detector — so Phase 2-5 have something concrete (and honest) to beat. Surface any data-quality problems *before* modeling, so future "wins" can be distinguished from dataset artifacts.

## Research & References

1. **Li et al., 2023 — HaluEval: A Large-Scale Hallucination Evaluation Benchmark for LLMs** (EMNLP). [arXiv:2305.11747](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.11747) · [GitHub](https://github.com/RUCAIBox/HaluEval).
   - Released the 35K benchmark (QA / dialogue / summarization).
   - Reports **ChatGPT @ 62.59% accuracy** and **GPT-4 @ 74.62% accuracy** on the QA split under their zero-shot judge protocol.
   - Establishes balanced binary classification as the canonical eval. → I use the QA split (10K rows × 2 answers = 20K balanced samples).

2. **Hugging Face Hallucinations Leaderboard** (2024 blog): [huggingface.co/blog/leaderboard-hallucinations](https://huggingface.co/blog/leaderboard-hallucinations). NLI-based detectors achieve **AUROC ≈ 0.88** on HaluEval. → Sets the bar Phase 2-5 must clear.

3. **EdinburghNLP — Awesome-Hallucination-Detection** (2024-2025): [github.com/EdinburghNLP/awesome-hallucination-detection](https://github.com/EdinburghNLP/awesome-hallucination-detection). Survey of detector families — NLI entailment, token-probability classifiers, retrieval-overlap, self-consistency. → My Phase 1 "industry-standard" baseline is a lexical-overlap heuristic (cheap stand-in for the NLI / retrieval-overlap families).

4. **Bang et al., 2025 — HalluLens: LLM Hallucination Benchmark** (ACL 2025): [aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.1176](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.1176.pdf). Distinguishes *factuality* (TruthfulQA) from *grounding* (HaluEval). HaluEval-QA is a **grounding** benchmark — the source knowledge is provided. → Phase 2 feature engineering should focus on *knowledge ↔ answer* relationships, not world facts.

### How research shaped today's experiments
- Use HaluEval-QA over TruthfulQA (the latter has known gold-answer errors and is now saturated due to training-set contamination per HalluLens analysis).
- Report **macro F1** as primary; track ROC-AUC, balanced accuracy, precision, recall as secondaries. macro F1 is honest under class-collapse, equivalent to accuracy when classes are balanced, and aligns with what published benchmarks report.
- Include a length-only baseline because EDA of *any* LLM-vs-LLM-generated dataset must probe length leakage before trusting deeper models.

### Critical data-quality finding (Phase 1 headline)

**Severe answer-length asymmetry:**

| Field | mean chars | median | max |
|-------|-----------:|-------:|----:|
| `right_answer` (grounded) | **14** | 12 | 529 |
| `hallucinated_answer` | **66** | 58 | 380 |
| ratio | **4.7×** | 4.8× | — |

The grounded answers are HotpotQA entity strings ("Arthur's Magazine"); the hallucinated answers are full sentences generated by ChatGPT ("First for Women was started first."). Any classifier with access to the answer text can partially solve the task by counting characters. **This is a dataset-construction artifact, not a property of real-world hallucinations.**

## Experiments

### Experiment 1.1 — Majority class (chance floor)
**Hypothesis:** macro F1 ≈ 0.33 (a constant majority predictor collapses one class).
**Method:** `DummyClassifier(strategy='most_frequent')`.
**Result:** acc 0.500, macro F1 0.333, ROC-AUC 0.500. As expected — the floor.

### Experiment 1.2 — Length-only LogReg (artifact probe)
**Hypothesis:** Length alone is a strong signal due to the 4.7× length asymmetry.
**Method:** 4 features: `answer_len`, `answer_tokens`, `question_len`, `knowledge_len`, all standardized. Logistic regression, default C, max_iter=2000.
**Result:** **acc 0.944, macro F1 0.944, ROC-AUC 0.971**, fit time 0.015 s. Scaled coefficients: `answer_len=+1.84`, `answer_tokens=+8.13`, `question_len=+0.30`, `knowledge_len=−0.16` — i.e. the model is overwhelmingly looking at "is the answer a long string of tokens or a short entity?"
**Interpretation:** The dataset is largely solved by length. This is the single most important Phase 1 finding.

### Experiment 1.3 — TF-IDF on answer only
**Hypothesis:** Bag-of-words on the answer captures length-correlated lexical patterns (function words, sentence connectives) in addition to genuine fabrication patterns.
**Method:** `TfidfVectorizer(ngram_range=(1,2), min_df=3, max_features=30k, sublinear_tf=True)` → LogReg.
**Result:** acc 0.919, macro F1 0.919, ROC-AUC 0.968, fit time 0.31 s.
**Interpretation:** Strong but *below* the length-only model. The TF-IDF feature set is partly redundant with length (longer strings have more non-zero TF-IDF features) and partly genuine — but the genuine signal is small.

### Experiment 1.4 — TF-IDF on question + answer
**Hypothesis:** Adding the question text should help (context for what counts as a valid answer).
**Method:** Same TF-IDF setup over the concatenated `question + [SEP] + answer` text.
**Result:** acc 0.789, macro F1 0.788, ROC-AUC 0.866, fit time 3.47 s.
**Interpretation:** **Adding the question hurt by −13 F1 points.** Why? Because both samples in a pair share the same question, the question tokens become non-discriminative noise that dilutes the answer-length signal. This is a clean parallel to the Keeper finding that "adding profile text hurts." More features ≠ better.

### Experiment 1.5 — Lexical-overlap threshold (industry-standard proxy)
**Hypothesis:** Fraction of answer tokens present in the knowledge passage is the cheapest stand-in for the NLI / retrieval-overlap family of detectors. Low overlap → likely hallucinated.
**Method:** Compute `|answer_tokens ∩ knowledge_tokens| / |answer_tokens|` (alpha tokens only, length > 2). Tune the threshold on **train** to maximize macro F1 (sweeping 101 thresholds from 0.0 to 1.0). Best train threshold: 0.95. Use `1 - overlap` as the probability score for AUROC.
**Result:** acc 0.714, macro F1 0.703, ROC-AUC 0.593, fit time 0 s.
**Interpretation:** This is **the most honest baseline** — it cannot exploit the length artifact because both classes' answers contain similar fractions of in-passage tokens. The 0.593 AUROC reveals that grounding *itself* is a weak signal at the surface level for this dataset; you really do need either NLI or deeper semantics to do better — which is exactly what Phase 2-5 will probe. This is the number to beat in Phase 2.

## Error analysis (best baseline)

Confusion matrix at threshold 0.5 (length_only_logreg, test n=4,000):
- True grounded → pred grounded: 1,895 / pred hallucinated: 105 (FPR 5.3%)
- True hallucinated → pred grounded: 131 / pred hallucinated: 1,869 (FNR 6.6%)

Errors look symmetric and uncorrelated with question topic — they correspond to the small overlap region of the length distributions (grounded answers that happen to be longer-than-typical 50+ char strings; hallucinated answers that are short single-clause sentences).

## Key Findings (post-worthy material)

1. **The HaluEval-QA benchmark is mostly solvable by counting characters.** A 4-feature length-only LogReg hits **94.4% accuracy** vs. ChatGPT zero-shot's published **62.6%**. The benchmark is testing length-classification, not hallucination detection. Any Phase 2+ result on raw HaluEval must come with a length-matched-control number, or it is not measuring what its authors think it is.

2. **Adding features hurt.** Adding question text to a TF-IDF answer model dropped macro F1 from 0.919 to 0.788 (−13 points). This mirrors the Keeper finding that "consensus projection destroys taste" — more inputs ≠ better, especially when the new inputs are shared across both classes and dilute the discriminative signal.

3. **The NLI-proxy baseline is the only honest one.** Lexical overlap between answer and knowledge gets 0.703 macro F1 — *below* the trivial length classifier, *above* the majority floor. This is the number Phase 2 must beat to claim it's doing real grounding work rather than length-pattern-matching.

## What didn't work / what I didn't build today
- No length-matched test set yet (planned for Phase 2). Today's baselines all evaluate on the raw HaluEval split, length artifact and all — by design, because Phase 1 is about *exposing* the artifact, not correcting for it.
- No deep models. Phase 2 will introduce sentence-transformer embeddings, a tuned cross-encoder NLI, and a small fine-tuned head — at which point I'll re-run on both the raw and length-matched splits.
